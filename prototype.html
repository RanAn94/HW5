Cash Flow Case

Pro forma earnings would affect the shareholders and investors’ judgment to the companies’ real financial state in the fiscal year. Pro forma earnings mean non-GAAP earnings, which usually do not reflect “bad stuff.” Pro forma earnings have no standard, and most time pro forma earnings look better than GAAP earnings. They are more permanent and could help investor to know the companies’ future earning power (Larcker & Tayan, 2010). However, pro forma earnings are still not a good financial earning statement for the public, because they would make it difficult for the public to read and analyze the real financial statements of companies.
